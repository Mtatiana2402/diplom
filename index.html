CITY OF LOVE

Malang is the second largest city in East Java province, Indonesia. It has a history dating back to the ancient Mataram Kingdom. The city is famous for its cool temperature. People in East Java sometimes call it "Paris of East Java.

Batu

is a city located in East Java Province of Indonesia. It is situated about 20 km to the northwest of Malang. Formerly, it was a part of Malang Regency; but in 2001, Batu became an independent city. A population of 190,000 people, it lies on the southern slopes of Gunung Welirang. Its population largely consists of Javanese.

Ijen

Ijen volcano (Kawah Ijen, Kawah Ijen) located on the Ijen plateau of the same name on the eastern edge of Java. Tourists come here to see the fascinating blue flame of burning sulfur, the world's largest acid lake in the night, and meet the sunrise on the crest of the crater.

Semeru

Semeru or Muhomer is the highest volcano on the island of Java in Indonesia. Semeru is located at the southern end of the volcanic complex - the Tengger Caldera. In the crater of the volcano is a lava lake.

Jl. Besar Ijen

Malang is a historical city and there are many historical buildings in it, and even a whole historical quarter located in the Jl. Besar Ijen. Along Jl. Besar Ijen, also called Ijen Boulevard, houses the largest number of colonial architecture buildings that have survived. Among the buildings especially pay attention to the old library, the Catholic Church, and the military museum Brawijaya, which is worth a visit.

The Panatran

Botanical Garden

Bogor Botanical Garden (Kebun Raya Bogor) is located in the Indonesian city of Bogor (West Java Province) and its surroundings, at the foot of the volcano Salak. It is the oldest and largest botanical garden in Indonesia, one of the oldest and largest in the world. In the collection of about 14.5 thousand plants 5839 species. . In 1941 another branch of the garden was opened - near the settlement of Purvodadi (Purworadi) on East Java, covering an area of 85 hectares. His specialization has become less moisture-loving plants.

Village Jodipan

Tourism Village Jodipan is a small area of the city in which students and artists painted all the houses in different colors and painted many 3D paintings on the walls. There are always a lot of tourists, there are cafes and restaurants, trays with food, souvenirs and goods for tourists. In general, a good place to take a photo and leave the memory of your visit to Malang.

Museum Panji

Museum Panji is the former Malang Tempo Doeloe museum, which was located in the city center but in 2017 was moved to a new place far beyond the Malang line. He tells about the history of the region and the city, i.e. in our opinion, this is a local history museum, and a very good one. It is very convenient to visit him on the way to the volcano Bromo, because It is located just on the road to it, 12 kilometers from the center. And the most interesting is that the museum has a swimming pool, and you can swim in it.

Badut Temple

Badut Temple or Candi Badut are another ruins of a Hindu temple. This is a historic place dating from 760, but at the same time it is revered by believers. During Hindu holidays, ceremonies and festivals take place here. From a tourist point of view, you can visit it, especially since it is located within the city limits, on its outskirts, but you will spend no more than 15 minutes on the inspection.

Masjid Tiban Mosque

Very beautiful multi-level mosque, or rather the whole complex, including shops and cafes. The mosque is new, is still being completed, and there are several exhibitions on the territory dedicated to its construction and architecture. Tourists can walk around the territory completely free, but when visiting they should dress appropriately: no short skirts and shorts, open shoulders, women should cover their heads.

Kidal Temple

Kidal Temple or Candi Kidal - the ruins of a Hindu temple, built in 1248 (according to other sources in 1260) for the burial of the second king of the kingdom of Singosari - king Anusapati. The place will be interesting to lovers of antiquities, but you will spend no more than 10 minutes on the inspection, especially since there is a chicken farm nearby, from which a terrible smell spreads.

Balekambang Beach

At 70 kilometers south of Malang are several large beaches of the Balekambang coast. Sunsets aren’t the only time to enjoy the Balekambang Beach. During low tide, the sea retreats, unveiling the coral stones that extend beyond the sand, flaunting the colours of its coral flora. Go ahead and do a little exploration, and let brightly coloured fish tickle your feet, as they weave in and out of the coral garden!

The Singosari Temple

The Singosari Temple or Candi Singosari is one of the last Hindu temples built by the great Singosari dynasty ruling East Java before the arrival of Islam on the island. It dates back to 1304 AD. Today it is a protected historical landmark. This is not a complex of temples, but only the historical ruins of one temple, surrounded by ancient statues. Also pay attention to the gate-statue on the road at the entrance to the temple. It also offers a great view of the surrounding mountains and the volcano Bromo. It is recommended to visit this temple in conjunction with the temple of Sumberawan, because He is not far from here.

Museum Brawijaya

Museum Brawijaya is a military history museum dedicated to the struggle of the Indonesian people for independence at the beginning of the 20th century. The state museum is not very well-groomed, but you should definitely look here while walking along the historic street of the city.

Bengkung Beach

Up the ante with a natural beach jacuzzi – Pantai Batu Bengkung. Get a little more intimate with nature with your very own private jacuzzi on a beach. Nope, it’s not the kind of jacuzzi you might be imagining right now! Instead, this beautiful rocky beach on the south coast of Malang transforms into an area of small natural saltwater pools at high tide. This is also a good time to swim in these pools, as the sea can be get pretty rough and unsuitable for swimming.

The beach also has the most stunning sunset views one could hope for. The beach is about a 2-3 hours ride away from Malang. Follow the directions to beach Sendangbiru but turn towards Goa Cina beach and beach Bajulmati. Pantai Batu Bengkung is about 2kms from beach Bajulmati.

Jago Temple

Jago Temple or Candi Jago - the ruins of a Buddhist temple, built in the period from 1268 to 1280 years. Although originally it was a Buddhist temple, later typical statues of Hindu deities appeared here. In our opinion, of all the temples in the vicinity of Malang, this is the most ambitious and worthy of a visit. It can be visited on the way to the volcano Bromo or on the way back, because he is on this route.

Pantai Mbehi can put all the other pretty beaches to shame with its turquoise waters, white sands and beautiful protected forests that it’s located beyond. Pantai Mbehi is approximately 3km from Kondang Merak Beach.

Ngliyep Beach

Feel the touch of soft white sand against your feet: Ngliyep Beach. Ngliyep beach is named because of its soft white sand. There is a small island, Mount Kombang, which can be accessed via a wooden bridge. It is also a spectacular spot where you can watch the sunrise and sunset. You can also head over to Bukit Cinta Kasih, translated as “Hill of Love”. There are myths that young people who come to this place would become a couple.

From the beach, you can also walk up a small hill to get to Teluk Putri, translated as Princess Bay. Its name is inspired by the silky soft smooth skin of the princess. Head 62 km towards the south of the city of Malang. You can travel through Donomulyo, or through Sumbermaning Kulon.

Goa-Cina Beach

Goa Cina Beach might not be as famous as other beaches in Malang, East Java. That probably has something to do with its tough access and dangerously strong currents. The ferocious swells of the Indian Ocean crash on three small islands near the shore. There is a rare natural phenomenon taking place in its very water. The tides from different directions meet in one point: between Nyonya Island and Bantengan Island.

Sumberawan Temple

Sumberawan Temple or Candi Sumberawan - one more ruin, only already of a Buddhist stupa. Its construction dates back to 1359, and allegedly it was timed to the visit of the great king Khayam Wuruk, the 4th ruler of the state Majapahit, the last pre-Islamic kingdom of Indonesia. The stupa itself is nothing remarkable, although it will certainly be interesting to lovers of antiquities. But the very path to it, which runs through the village quarters and fields, as well as along the canal, which the locals use for bathing and streeks, is interesting.
